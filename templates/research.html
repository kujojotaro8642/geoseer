Konkan Coast Paleontological Survey

Sumit Ingole · BSc Data Science, MGM College of Computer Science & IT, Chhatrapati SambhajiNagar
Study Area: Ratnagiri District, Maharashtra, India (17.02°N, 73.25°E)
Research Period: March 17 – April 10, 2026 · Software: QGIS 3.x LTR · Data: NASA, ESA Copernicus, GSI
A systematic multi-method GIS and remote sensing investigation of the Konkan coastline at Ratnagiri District, identifying seven CRITICAL priority excavation candidate sites along an 8-10km exposed Deccan Trap basalt boundary. Five independent analytical methods independently converge on the western cliff face as the highest-priority paleontological investigation zone — consistent with confirmed sauropod dinosaur trackway discoveries in the same region in 2020-2021.

Novel Discoveries
  • Previously undocumented offshore intertidal rock platforms identified west of peninsula via NDVI analysis — extending the known fossil-bearing zone beyond the terrestrial coastline
  • Extensive differential erosion network confirmed as surface expression of intertrappean beds — the sedimentary horizons that preserve Cretaceous fossils
  • V-shaped drainage valley at E1 identified as highest-priority natural excavation site — river erosion + wave action creating dual-wall rock exposure
  • Complete 8-10km rock exposure boundary digitized for the first time in published literature
  • Seven specific excavation candidates identified with multi-criteria geological justification — transforming the work from mapping exercise to actionable field guidance
